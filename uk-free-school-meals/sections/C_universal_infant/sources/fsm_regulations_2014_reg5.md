# The Education (Free School Meals) (England) Regulations 2014 — Regulation 5

## Regulation 5 — Universal entitlement for pupils in reception class or years 1 or 2

Every registered pupil at a relevant school who is in—
(a) the reception class;
(b) year 1; or
(c) year 2

is entitled to a free school lunch without reference to the qualifying criteria in regulation 4.

This entitlement is in addition to and does not replace the means-tested entitlement under regulation 4. It applies regardless of household income, benefit status, or any other criterion.

## Year group definitions

For the purposes of these regulations—
- "Reception class" (also "Year R") means the first year of compulsory schooling, typically for children who are 4–5 years old;
- "Year 1" means the second year of primary school, typically for children who are 5–6 years old;
- "Year 2" means the third year of primary school, typically for children who are 6–7 years old.

Children in Year 3 and above are not covered by the universal entitlement under this Regulation and must instead meet a qualifying criterion under Regulation 4.

**Cross-reference:** Regulation 5 implements the statutory duty imposed by section 105 of the Children and Families Act 2014. The Children and Families Act creates the obligation; Regulation 5 sets out the operative rule.
