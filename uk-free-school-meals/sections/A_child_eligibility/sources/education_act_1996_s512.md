# Education Act 1996 — Section 512, 512ZA

## Section 512 — Provision of meals

**512 Provision of meals etc.**

(1) A local authority in England shall provide—
(a) for registered pupils at schools maintained by it who are within a prescribed description, free milk, meals or other refreshments; and
(b) for registered pupils at such schools who are not within that description, milk, meals or other refreshments.

(2) Where a local authority provides meals under subsection (1)(b), it shall do so for pupils whose parents wish them to have meals at school on such occasions and at such a place as the authority considers appropriate.

## Section 512ZA — Prescribed descriptions of pupils

The regulations prescribe that the following pupils are entitled to free school meals provided under section 512(1)(a):

- A registered pupil at a relevant school who is of compulsory school age.
- A registered pupil at a sixth form who is aged 16, 17 or 18 and meets a qualifying criterion.

Compulsory school age begins at the start of the term following a child's fifth birthday, but in practice the provision applies from the start of the Reception year (age 4–5).

**Relevant school** means—
(a) a school maintained by a local authority in England;
(b) an Academy;
(c) a non-maintained special school;
(d) a pupil referral unit.

Independent schools (fee-paying) and home-educated children are not within scope of this provision.

## School Standards and Framework Act 1998, Section 114

A "maintained school" includes community schools, voluntary aided schools, voluntary controlled schools, foundation schools, and community special schools maintained by a local authority in England.
