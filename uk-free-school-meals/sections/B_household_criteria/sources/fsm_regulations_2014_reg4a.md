# The Education (Free School Meals) (England) Regulations 2014 — Regulation 4A

## Regulation 4A — Looked-after children and care leavers

A person is also within a qualifying criterion if the person—
(a) is looked after by a local authority within the meaning of section 22 of the Children Act 1989; or
(b) has left care and is a "care leaver" as defined in section 23A or 23C of the Children Act 1989.

Looked-after children and care leavers are entitled to free school meals without reference to the household's income or benefit receipt.

## Children Act 1989 — Relevant definitions

**Section 22 — General duty of local authority in relation to children looked after by them**

A child is "looked after by a local authority" if—
(a) the authority is providing accommodation for the child under section 20; or
(b) the child is subject to a care order.

**Section 23A — The responsible authority and relevant children**

A "relevant child" for care leaver purposes means a child who—
(a) is not being looked after by any local authority;
(b) was, before last ceasing to be looked after, an eligible child for the purposes of paragraph 19B of Schedule 2; and
(c) is aged 16 or 17.

**Section 23C — Continuing functions in respect of former relevant children**

A "former relevant child" (care leaver) means a person who has been a relevant child and is aged 18 or above, continuing to receive support from the responsible authority.

**Note:** Regulation 4A qualifies without any reference to income, benefit receipt, or any other criterion in Regulation 4. It is an unconditional entitlement — if the child is looked after or a care leaver, Section B is satisfied regardless of household finances.
