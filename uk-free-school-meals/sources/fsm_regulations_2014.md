# The Education (Free School Meals) (England) Regulations 2014 (as amended 2018, 2023)

## Regulation 3 — Persons who are entitled to free school lunches

(1) A person is entitled to a free school lunch if the person is a registered pupil at a relevant school in England and is aged under 16, or is aged 16 to 18 and is a relevant sixth-form pupil.

(2) For the purposes of this regulation—
- "registered pupil" has the same meaning as in section 434 of the Education Act 1996;
- "relevant school" means a school within the categories set out in section 512ZA of the Education Act 1996;
- a child is considered to be "aged under 16" based on their age at the start of the school year.

## Regulation 4 — Qualifying criteria

A person is within a qualifying criterion for the purposes of regulation 3 if the person, or a person with whom the person lives, is in receipt of—

(a) Universal Credit, provided that the household's net earnings do not exceed £7,400 per year (this figure is net earnings after deduction of income tax and national insurance contributions, as calculated by the Universal Credit system);

(b) Income Support under the Social Security Contributions and Benefits Act 1992;

(c) income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA) under the Jobseekers Act 1995;

(d) income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) under the Welfare Reform Act 2007;

(e) Child Tax Credit, provided that—
    (i) the person is not entitled to Working Tax Credit; and
    (ii) the annual gross income of the person as calculated by Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs does not exceed £16,190;

(f) support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999 (support provided by the National Asylum Support Service — NASS);

(g) the Guarantee Credit element of Pension Credit under the State Pension Credit Act 2002 (applies to pupils aged 16–18 in sixth form).

## Regulation 4A — Looked-after children and care leavers

A person is also within a qualifying criterion if the person—
(a) is looked after by a local authority within the meaning of section 22 of the Children Act 1989; or
(b) has left care and is a "care leaver" as defined in section 23A or 23C of the Children Act 1989.

Looked-after children and care leavers are entitled to free school meals without reference to the household's income or benefit receipt.

## Regulation 5 — Universal entitlement for pupils in reception class or years 1 or 2

Every registered pupil at a relevant school who is in—
(a) the reception class;
(b) year 1; or
(c) year 2

is entitled to a free school lunch without reference to the qualifying criteria in regulation 4.

This entitlement is in addition to and does not replace the means-tested entitlement under regulation 4. It applies regardless of household income, benefit status, or any other criterion.

## Guidance Note — Universal Credit income threshold

The £7,400 annual net earnings threshold applies to the net earned income of all members of the household. This is the figure used in the Universal Credit calculation, not the household's total income. Unearned income (savings, investments, rental) is not included in this threshold calculation.

The threshold applies at the time of the most recent UC monthly assessment period. A family that has recently stopped receiving UC remains eligible until the end of the current academic year.

## Year group definitions

For the purposes of these regulations—
- "Reception class" (also "Year R") means the first year of compulsory schooling, typically for children who are 4–5 years old;
- "Year 1" means the second year of primary school, typically for children who are 5–6 years old;
- "Year 2" means the third year of primary school, typically for children who are 6–7 years old.

Children in Year 3 and above are not covered by the universal entitlement and must meet a qualifying criterion under Regulation 4.
